# Preparing for Your Doctor Visit

## Before Your Appointment

### Gather Your Information
- **Current medications** (including over-the-counter and supplements)
- **Medical history** and previous test results
- **Family medical history**
- **Insurance cards** and photo ID
- **List of questions** you want to ask
- **Symptom diary** if tracking ongoing issues

### Organize Your Questions
Write down your questions in order of importance:
- What is my main concern?
- What symptoms am I experiencing?
- What treatments are available?
- What are the risks and benefits?
- How will this affect my daily life?

### Prepare Your Medical History
- Recent hospitalizations or ER visits
- Changes in medications
- New symptoms or concerns
- Vaccination status
- Recent travel (if relevant)

## During Your Appointment

### Communication Tips
- **Be specific** about symptoms (when they started, frequency, severity)
- **Bring up concerns** even if they seem minor
- **Ask for clarification** if you don't understand something
- **Take notes** or bring someone to help remember details
- **Be honest** about lifestyle factors (diet, exercise, smoking, alcohol)

### Key Questions to Ask
- "Can you explain that in simpler terms?"
- "What are my treatment options?"
- "What are the risks and benefits of each option?"
- "What should I expect during treatment?"
- "When should I follow up?"
- "What symptoms should I watch for?"

### Understanding Your Diagnosis
- Ask for written materials or reliable websites
- Request a summary of the visit
- Clarify next steps and timeline
- Understand when to seek immediate care

## After Your Appointment

### Follow-Up Actions
- **Schedule** any recommended tests or procedures
- **Fill prescriptions** and understand how to take them
- **Make lifestyle changes** as discussed
- **Track symptoms** if monitoring a condition
- **Prepare** for next appointment

### When to Call Back
- Worsening symptoms
- Side effects from medications
- Questions about your treatment plan
- Need for clarification on instructions
- Concerns about your condition

## Making the Most of Limited Time

### Prioritize Your Concerns
- List your top 3-5 concerns
- Start with the most important
- Be concise but complete
- Don't leave major concerns for the end

### Bring Support
- **Family member or friend** to help remember details
- **Advocate** if you have complex needs
- **Translator** if needed for language barriers

## Special Considerations

### For New Patients
- Arrive early to complete paperwork
- Bring complete medical records
- Prepare a comprehensive health history
- Ask about office policies and procedures

### For Chronic Conditions
- Track symptoms between visits
- Bring test results from other providers
- Discuss treatment effectiveness
- Address quality of life concerns

## Insurance and Costs

### Before Your Visit
- Verify your insurance coverage
- Understand your copay and deductible
- Ask about payment plans if needed
- Inquire about generic medication options

### Questions About Costs
- "What will this test/procedure cost?"
- "Are there less expensive alternatives?"
- "Does my insurance cover this treatment?"
- "Can I get a cost estimate before treatment?"

*Source: Agency for Healthcare Research and Quality, American Medical Association*
